Local Favorites

Places Worth Every Mile

These are the spots locals actually go — and where our designs were born. Swipe through for trip ideas, then grab the art to remember them by.

Deception Pass
Whidbey Island · Oak Harbor

Dramatic tidal strait, old-growth forest hikes, and sunset views that never get old. Our most-visited backyard.

★★★★★ · Local favorite since 1933
Mount Rainier NP
Pierce County · 3.5 hrs from Oak Harbor

The crown of the Cascades. Wildflower meadows in July, alpenglow year-round, and hiking trails for every level.

★★★★★ · 1.5M visitors/year
San Juan Islands
Friday Harbor · Ferry from Anacortes

Kayak with orcas, eat fresh Dungeness crab, watch eagles from limestone bluffs. Pure Pacific Northwest magic.

★★★★★ · Top WA whale-watching spot
Hoh Rainforest
Olympic Peninsula · 3 hrs from Oak Harbor

One of the few temperate rainforests on Earth. Massive moss-draped maples, Roosevelt elk, Hall of Mosses trail.

★★★★★ · UNESCO Biosphere Reserve
Olympic Coast
Rialto Beach · Olympic NP

Sea stacks, tide pools, drift log beaches, and purple sunsets over the open Pacific. No phone signal — just wild beauty.

★★★★★ · Tide pool heaven
Diablo Lake & Dam
North Cascades NP · Newhalem

Surreal turquoise glacial lake formed by the historic Diablo Dam — the color will stop you in your tracks. Darkest skies in Washington for stargazing.

★★★★★ · Best dark sky in WA state
Ebey's Landing
Coupeville · Whidbey Island

Dramatic bluffs, a historic ferry crossing, and wide open prairie views. Minutes from Oak Harbor — perfect for a morning walk.

★★★★★ · National Historical Reserve
Anacortes Ferry
Anacortes · Gateway to islands

Jump on a ferry to Lopez, Orcas, or Shaw island for a weekend of bikes, bays, and blissful slow living in the Salish Sea.

★★★★★ · Locals' island escape
